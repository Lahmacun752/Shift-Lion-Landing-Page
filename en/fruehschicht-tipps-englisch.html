Why early shifts feel so exhausting

During an early shift, your body often has to become active before it is fully awake. It becomes especially difficult if you go to bed too late, start the morning in a rush or have to get out of bed without a clear plan.

1. Prepare the evening before

The best early shift does not start in the morning, but the evening before. Everything you prepare in the evening saves you stress in the morning.

Evening checklist

  • Lay out your clothes
  • Pack your work bag
  • Prepare food
  • Set your alarm
  • Put your phone away early enough

2. Use the right alarm routine

A single alarm right next to your bed is risky. A clear alarm routine is better because it actually gets you out of bed.

Practical alarm routine

  • first alarm: wake up
  • second alarm: get up
  • do not place your phone directly next to the bed
  • turn on the light immediately

3. Breakfast before an early shift

Not everyone can eat a lot early in the morning. The important thing is not to start your shift completely empty. A small and simple breakfast is often enough.

Simple ideas

  • Yogurt with oats
  • Banana and coffee
  • Whole grain bread with egg or cheese
  • Protein shake when time is short

4. Use coffee wisely

Coffee can help, but it does not replace sleep. Use it intentionally instead of drinking it constantly throughout the day. Too much coffee can make your sleep worse later.

Frequently Asked Questions About Early Shifts

What helps against tiredness during early shifts?

The most helpful things are enough sleep, preparation the evening before, morning light and a fixed routine.

Should you eat breakfast before an early shift?

A small breakfast is often useful. It does not have to be large, but a little energy can make the start easier.

How do you prepare for an early shift?

Clothes, food, your work bag and alarms should be ready the evening before. This makes the morning calmer.
